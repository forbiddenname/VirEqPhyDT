Abstract

Digital Twin (DT) technology, which enables real-time spatiotemporal mapping of physical objects, offers a promising way to provide embodied agents with dynamic, reality-mirrored virtual learning environment. However, most existing agent-DT systems either rely on heavy 3D point-cloud reconstruction or on loosely coupled geometric-parameter synchronization, making it difficult to efficiently maintain geometric dynamic equivalence between complex indoor scenes and their virtual counterparts.

This paper introduces VirEqPhy, a geometric dynamic equivalence method that formulates virtual-physical consistency as a voxel-based multi-task cascade that jointly perform CAD model retrieval and 9-DoF pose estimation from a single RGB image. VirEqPhy embeds observed objects and CAD models into a shared voxel feature space, couples a two-stage rotation estimation scheme with Procrustes-based refinement, and optionally integrates a weakly supervised Top-k retrieval module that learns object-CAD associations from multi-similarity labels without dense manual correspondences.

In the supervised setting, VirEqPhy achieves 24.2% high-threshold mean AP for model retrieval and 24.4% class-level alignment accuracy under the Scan2CAD criterion. In the weakly supervised setting, VirEqPhy achieves 29.2% high-threshold mean AP and maintains strong performance on unseen categories. In both settings, it surpasses representative baselines. A real-robot indoor navigation experiment further verifies its effectiveness in dynamic real-world environments.
